4 Rate of Change & Devaluation Timeline

How frequently Indian banks update reward rates, modify caps, add/remove portal merchants, and change exclusions.

Pattern: The Indian credit card community actively optimizes rewards (routing insurance, rent, tax payments through cards). Banks like HDFC respond by closing these gaps — creating a cat-and-mouse dynamic that drives 3-6 rule changes per year per major issuer.

🗓️ Major Devaluation Timeline (2024–2026)

Apr 2024
Axis Bank — Massive devaluation across Atlas, Magnus, Privilege. Group A/B transfer caps introduced. Accelerated categories removed from fuel, insurance, gold, jewellery.
Oct 2024
HDFC Bank — Loyalty program and reward point redemption limits revised for specific cards. Amex-Vistara merger eliminates Club Vistara as Amex transfer partner.
Nov 2024
ICICI Bank — No rewards on government transactions. Fee structure revisions across multiple cards. SBI revises finance charges to 3.75% per month.
Feb 2025
ICICI Bank — Launches iShop Rewards 360 portal (positive change). HDFC SmartBuy continues as benchmark competitor.
May 2024
IDFC FIRST — Major devaluation: accelerated online rewards discontinued, base reward rate reduced on Wealth/Select cards.
Jun 2025
HDFC — Lounge access rules change. No longer automatic — spend thresholds required. Amex removes fuel from Membership Rewards earning.
Jul 2025
HDFC — 1% fee on rent payments, wallet loads, online gaming. Insurance reward caps by card tier (2K–10K RP/month).
Jan 2026
HDFC — Broader fee/access overhaul. Annual/renewal fees adjusted. Lounge access fully spend-linked. IDFC FIRST — Int'l spend rewards cut to 5X (from 10X), base rate moved to ₹200/point.
Feb 2026
HDFC Infinia — Redemption capped at 5 times/month, 50K points/month for statement credit, ₹1.5L/month for flights/hotels.
May 2026
HDFC Regalia Gold — Base earn rate lowered from 4RP/₹150 to 5RP/₹200. Lounge access requires ₹60K quarterly spend from July 2026. Kotak — Cash value revised from ₹0.25 to ₹0.20 per point.
System design implication: Reward rules change 3-8 times per year per issuer, with HDFC being the most volatile. An extraction system needs continuous monitoring — not just a one-time scrape. Monitoring bank MITC update dates, SmartBuy/iShop pages, and enthusiast blogs (CardExpert, CardInsider) for breaking changes is critical.

🚫 Common Exclusion Categories

MCC codes that earn 0 points across most issuers:

Fuel (most cards) Rent payments (BBPS) Wallet loads (Paytm, MobiKwik) EMI conversions Cash advances Government txns Online gaming Gold/Jewellery (Axis) Insurance (capped) Education (via 3rd party) Railways (IDFC capped) Utility bills (above threshold)
Extraction challenge: Exclusions are the hardest to extract — they're buried in MITC PDFs and rarely listed on product pages. IDFC FIRST is a notable exception, earning RP on rent, utility and insurance.

🔴 Highest Staleness Risk

Sources that fall behind first:

BankBazaar product pages2–6 months lag
PaisaBazaar comparison tables1–3 months lag
Bank's own product feature pages2–8 weeks lag
Wishfin, Fincash, generic aggregators6+ months lag

Aggregators are lead-gen businesses — they update fee data (for affiliate conversions) faster than reward details.

🟡 Common Data Conflicts

Point values: Wishfin says HDFC = ₹1/point; CardInsider says ₹0.50; actual varies by redemption (₹0.20 for catalog, ₹1.00 for SmartBuy flights)

Earn rates: Older articles cite 4RP/₹150 for Regalia Gold; changed to 5RP/₹200 in May 2026

Caps: MITC PDFs may state daily caps while product page shows monthly

Exclusions: Product page says "earn on all retail" while MITC excludes 15+ MCC categories

🟢 Most Reliable Sources

1. Bank MITC PDFs — Ground truth, but dense and unstandardized

2. SmartBuy accelerated-earn page — Structured, per-card multiplier table

3. CardExpert.in — Enthusiast-verified, 250+ cards, updated within days

4. CardTrail.in calculators — Hardcoded RP values per bank/card

5. TechnoFino community — First to spot changes, real data points

🧠 LLM-Assisted Extraction Flow

For unstructured sources (PDFs, blog articles, forum posts):

Step 1Extract text from PDF/HTML
Step 2Chunk into per-card, per-rule sections
Step 3LLM extracts structured fields
Step 4Validate against schema
Step 5Cross-reference with existing profile
Step 6Flag conflicts for human review
Step 7Merge confirmed changes
Key insight: LLMs are excellent at extracting "4 RP per ₹150" → {"rpPer": 4, "spendPer": 150} from natural language. The main challenge is detecting what changed between two versions of a T&C document and determining the effective date.
